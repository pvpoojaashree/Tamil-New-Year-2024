Tamil New Year

Tamil New Year is celebrated with a lot of devotion and dedication all over Tamil Nadu. Holds religious significance with legends of Lord Indra and Lord Brahma. Tamil New Year is celebrated on the first day of Tamil month Chitterai. This year name is Kurothi.

About

Puthandu is a prominent festival which is celebrated in Tamil Nadu. Puthandu is also referred to as Varusha Pirappu or Tamil New Year. Puthandu is Celebrated by Tamil People all over India. All Tamil People celebrate this day with great enthusiasm and fervor and hope for the prosperity and good fortune in the coming year. This day is celebrated on the first day of Tamil month Chitterai.It is believed that on this auspicious day, Lord Indra came to the earth on this day to ensure peace and serenity. It is also believed that Lord Brahma began the establishment of the universe.

Astrologically, this day is decided according to the position of the Sun. The Sun's position would be in the midway across the earth between the southern and northern hemisphere. That signifies the balance in between the people and the nature.

History

The ancient festival has its roots going back thousands of years during the Chola reign. It is believed that Puthandu was first celebrated during the 9th to the 13th centuries marking the beginning of the Tamil New Year. Puthandu also celebrates new beginnings, harvests and astronomical events.

Significance

Puthandu holds deep cultural and spiritual significance and symbolises hope and the cyclic nature of life. It also marks the beginningof the summer and the end of winter. During the celebration, families come together and perform rituals of cleansing and renewal. They also wear new clothes, and exchangev greetings of the new year, wishing each other health and prosperity. Homes are decorated with kolams and mango leaves as it is believed that these bring in postivity in the house and ward off evil spirits.
